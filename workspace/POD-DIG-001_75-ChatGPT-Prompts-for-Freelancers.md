# How To Use This Pack

Every prompt in this pack is ready to copy and paste directly into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini — no editing required. Each one is written to give you a useful, professional output on the first try.

To customise a prompt for your situation, simply paste it in, then follow up with a second message like: *"Make this shorter,"* *"Make it sound less formal,"* or *"Rewrite this for a design client instead."* That two-message approach gets you 90% of the way there without rebuilding anything from scratch. Work through each section as you need it, or bookmark the ones most relevant to where your business is right now.

# Bonus Tips: Getting More from Every Prompt

**Tip 1 — Give it context first.**
Before pasting a prompt, add one sentence of context: *"I'm a freelance illustrator who works with children's book publishers."* ChatGPT will tailor every response to your world without you needing to rewrite the prompt.

**Tip 2 — Use the follow-up.**
After any prompt, type: *"Give me three more versions of this"* or *"Make it half as long"* or *"Rewrite this in a more casual tone."* The follow-up message is where good outputs become great ones.

**Tip 3 — Save your best outputs.**
When a prompt gives you a response you love, save the original prompt plus the output. Over time, you'll build a personal library of things that work specifically for your business.

**Tip 4 — Ask for options.**
When you're not sure which direction to take, ask ChatGPT to write three versions — formal, casual, and direct — then choose the one that fits your voice. This is faster than editing one version back and forth.

**Tip 5 — Feed it your existing work.**
Paste in a previous email, proposal, or piece of copy you're proud of and say: *"Match this tone and style."* Then use any prompt in this pack. ChatGPT will write in your voice.

**Tip 6 — Use it to think, not just write.**
Don't just use these prompts for drafting. Use ChatGPT to think through decisions: *"I have a client who keeps changing the brief. Here's the situation. What are my options?"* It's a business thinking partner, not just a writing tool.
